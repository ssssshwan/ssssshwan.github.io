Sunghwan Kim

I am a first lieutenant at Agency for Defense Development (ADD), Korea's counterpart to U.S. DARPA, where I work on computer vision and machine learning. Previously, I received B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Mathematics (double major) at KAIST.

Email  /  CV  /  Linkedin  /  Google Scholar  /  Github

Research Interests

My research interests are in computer vision, especially efficient algorithms for visual perceptions. Currently, I am exploring methods to leverage pre-trained Foundation Models (FMs) in vision to enhance downstream tasks. Additionally, I am passionate about optimizing vision models with hardware efficiency.

Projects
Deep learning-based Application Program for UAVs
Agency for Defense Development (ADD), 2022-2023

We developed application program for UAVs that seamlessly integrates resource optimization by multi-threading, and I/O interfacing with flight control system. Also, we devised a test-time diversification method for time-series images to reduce the model's predictive uncertainty.
Model Acceleration on Edge Devices
Agency for Defense Development (ADD), 2022-2023

We implemented network compression techniques such as knowledge distillation, network pruning, and model quantization to accelerate models on edge devices like NPU, DPU on FPGA board. We were able to more than double the throughput by appling the techniques.

Object Detection in Infrared Imagery
Agency for Defense Development (ADD), 2021-2022

We developed real-time object detection algorithms for defense systems. We generated synthetic infrared imagery for training data and constructed the entire learning pipeline.
